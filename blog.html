Rome.ista Photography Page

by Luke Rebeiro

Posted on September 19th 2020

I have designed a photography website.

I designed this website from the ground up. I found the design process challenging at times it was a great experience to learn and develop my skills as a developer. I started off by drawing a flow-chart mapping out the different areas of every web page which will be created. On the website, I have included a home, about me, gallery and contents pages. Each page will have a navigation bar and footer bar with social media icons.

I started of my designs on paper as you can see from the image below

I used Adobe XD as my main design programme to create a wireframe on how the website is going to look once it is completed. As you can see from the screenshots below I have designed different layouts to go on to the different web pages. After wireframing my designs I used colours, logos and images to get an idea of how I wanted the website to look.

The pages went through alot of design changes throughout the development of this project because they were not user friendly and the overall experience was bad. By changing locations of images, navbar designs, gallery selection and layout it made the UI easy to use.

The about me page was a simple design which only needed a few pictures and some text which explained to the viewer who the photographer is as you can see from the image below this was the final design of the page.

Furthermore, the contact page was also another challenge to complete. I have linked an email to the web page directly for people to interact with. The form is used to interact with a view also as you can see from the form it is very basic which consists of a name, email, subject and message.

The gallery section of the website was the most challenging to complete. It went through a lot of design changes until I found the right one. As you can see I have used a lightbox in the final product of the gallery which makes the images interactive and the user is able to see the images more clearly which they click on the image. I used javascript to design the gallery and control the functions. .

Overall I am very pleased with the final design of the website and I followed the brief.
